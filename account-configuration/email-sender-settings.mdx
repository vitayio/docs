---
title: "Email sender settings"
description: "Control the From name and email address that references and candidates see when Vitay sends them emails."
---

Every email Vitay sends on your behalf — reference requests, reminders, and thank-you notes — shows a sender name and email address in the recipient's inbox. You can choose whether those come from the individual recruiter's identity or from a shared, firm-wide address.

<Note>
  You need a **root recruiter** (admin) role to change email sender settings. Changes take effect for all outgoing emails immediately after saving.
</Note>

## How sender identity works

Vitay offers two modes:

| Mode | From name shown | From address shown |
|---|---|---|
| **Recruiter identity** (default) | The assigned recruiter's name | The assigned recruiter's email |
| **Firm-wide override** | A generic name you configure | A generic address you configure |

You can mix these: for example, use the recruiter's real name but a shared company address.

## Change the sender name

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Configuration settings">
    Go to **Settings → Configuration** in the left-hand navigation.

    ![SCREENSHOT: Settings → Configuration page showing the email sender name section — placeholder](/images/placeholder.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the sender name controls">
    Look for the **Send Emails Using Assigned Recruiter's Name** toggle and the **From: Sender name** field beneath it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose recruiter name or a shared name">
    - To use each recruiter's own name, turn **Send Emails Using Assigned Recruiter's Name** **on**.
    - To send every email under the same display name, turn that toggle **off** and type your preferred name (for example, "Acme Recruiting Team") in the **From: Sender name** field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The change applies to all future outbound emails.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Change the sender email address

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Configuration settings">
    Go to **Settings → Configuration**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the sender email controls">
    Look for the **Send Emails Using Assigned Recruiter's Email** toggle and the **From: Email address** field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose recruiter address or a shared address">
    - To send from each recruiter's own address, turn **Send Emails Using Assigned Recruiter's Email** **on**.
    - To send from one shared address for your whole firm, turn that toggle **off** and enter the address (for example, `hiring@yourcompany.com`) in **From: Email address**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Deliverability and DMARC

If you set a custom From address on a domain you own (e.g. `hiring@yourcompany.com`), your domain's DMARC policy may cause emails to land in spam or be rejected outright — unless Vitay is authorised to send on your domain's behalf.

To resolve this, you need to publish DNS records that authorise Vitay as a sender. Contact **support@vitay.io** to get the specific records, then ask your IT team or domain registrar to add them. Once Vitay verifies the records, a green "valid" indicator appears on the **Developers** tab in Settings.

<Note>
  DMARC setup requires access to your company's DNS. It is a one-time step per domain. Vitay support can walk you through it.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="References say emails are landing in their spam folder">
    This is most commonly a deliverability issue. If you are using a custom From address, confirm that your DNS records (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) have been set up and verified in Vitay. If you are using a recruiter's personal email, ask your IT team whether your company's email domain has a restrictive DMARC policy that prevents third-party sending.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I saved a custom From address but outgoing emails still show the recruiter's address">
    Check that the **Send Emails Using Assigned Recruiter's Email** toggle is turned **off**. When the toggle is on, the recruiter's individual address always takes priority over the custom field.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
