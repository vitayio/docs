---
title: "Editing your answers after submitting"
description: "Whether you can change your answers once you've submitted the reference form, and what to do if you had trouble submitting."
---

Once you click **Submit** on the reference form, your answers are locked. There is no self-serve way to edit them after submission — the form closes to prevent duplicate or conflicting responses.

If you need a change made, the recruiter can unlock the reference on their end. Reach out to them and they'll sort it out.

## I submitted but need to change something

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the recruiter's contact details">
    Open the original reference request email you received from Vitay. The recruiter's name is in the email, and you can reply to reach them directly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explain what needs to change">
    Let the recruiter know which answer you'd like to correct and what the updated answer should be. They can either make the edit themselves or reopen the form for you.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## I'm having trouble submitting

If you're stuck on the form and can't get it to submit at all, try these steps first:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The form won't let me submit">
    Make sure all required questions are answered — required fields are usually marked. Scroll through the whole form to check nothing was missed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I get an error when I click Submit">
    Try refreshing the page and re-entering your answers. If the error says the reference has already been filled, your submission went through — no need to resubmit.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The form keeps timing out or crashing">
    Try a different browser (Google Chrome works best) or switch to your phone's mobile data instead of a work network. Some corporate networks block external links.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="None of the above worked">
    Reply to your original request email and let the recruiter know what's happening. They can resend the link or help troubleshoot.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
