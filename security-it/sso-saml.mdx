SAML single sign-on (SSO) lets your recruiters log in to Vitay through your
organisation's identity provider (IdP) — such as Okta, Azure AD, or Google
Workspace — without needing a separate Vitay password. Access is controlled
centrally from your IdP, so joiners, movers, and leavers are handled in one
place.

<Note>
  SSO is configured on a per-firm basis by Vitay's team. Reach out to
  [support@vitay.io](mailto:support@vitay.io) to get started. You will need an
  admin or IT role in your IdP to complete the setup on your side.
</Note>

## How Vitay's SAML SSO works

Vitay acts as the SAML **service provider (SP)**. Your IdP authenticates your
users and sends a signed SAML assertion back to Vitay. Vitay validates that
assertion and logs the user in — no password is exchanged with Vitay.

The flow is **IdP-initiated**: your users start from a tile or link in your IdP
dashboard and land directly in their Vitay account. Vitay uses the email address
in the SAML assertion (`NameID`) to match the user to their Vitay account.

<Note>
  The user's email address in Vitay must match the email your IdP sends in the
  SAML assertion. If a user's email changes in your IdP, update it in Vitay too.
</Note>

## What you provide to Vitay

To set up SSO, share the following with Vitay's team:

- Your IdP's **SAML metadata URL** (or the metadata XML file directly). This
  contains the SSO endpoint and signing certificate Vitay uses to validate
  assertions.
- The **email domains** that should be covered (for example, `acme.com`).
- Any **login URL or redirect** preferences for your environment.

## What Vitay provides to you

Vitay's team will give you:

- Vitay's **SP metadata** (entity ID and assertion consumer service URL) to
  enter in your IdP when creating the Vitay application.
- Confirmation of which **NameID format** to use — Vitay expects an
  email-address format NameID.

## Security notes

- SAML assertions are **cryptographically signed** by your IdP. Vitay validates
  the signature against your IdP's certificate before granting access.
- Vitay runs `ruby-saml` 1.18, which includes fixes for CVE-2024-45409 and
  related SAML signature-bypass vulnerabilities.
- Sessions are scoped to your firm's domain and expire after 24 hours, requiring
  re-authentication through your IdP.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="A user gets 'Unauthorized access' after being redirected back">
    The email in the SAML assertion doesn't match any active Vitay account in
    your firm. Confirm the user exists in Vitay with the exact same email address
    your IdP is sending. If the user is new, an admin needs to add them to Vitay
    first (or pair SSO with SCIM provisioning — see
    [SCIM user provisioning](/security-it/scim-provisioning)).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Login worked before but now fails with a signature error">
    Your IdP's signing certificate may have rotated. Provide Vitay's team with
    the updated IdP metadata URL or certificate so they can update the
    configuration.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Users are prompted for a Vitay password instead of being redirected">
    SSO is IdP-initiated — users must start from the application tile in your
    IdP, not from the Vitay login page. Check that the correct tile is configured
    and assigned to the user in your IdP.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
