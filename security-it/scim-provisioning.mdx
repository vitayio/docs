title: "SCIM user provisioning"
description: "Automatically provision and deprovision Vitay users from your identity provider using SCIM."
---

SCIM (System for Cross-domain Identity Management) lets your identity provider
(IdP) automatically create, update, and deactivate Vitay user accounts. When
paired with [SSO](/security-it/sso-saml), your IT team manages who has access to
Vitay entirely from the IdP — no manual account creation or cleanup needed.

<Note>
  SCIM provisioning is configured with Vitay's team as part of your SSO setup.
  Contact [support@vitay.io](mailto:support@vitay.io) to enable it. You will
  need an admin role in your IdP to configure the SCIM connector on your side.
</Note>

## What SCIM handles

| Event in your IdP | What happens in Vitay |
|---|---|
| New user assigned to Vitay | A Vitay account is created or activated for that user |
| User profile updated (name, email) | Vitay syncs the change to the user's account |
| User unassigned or deprovisioned | Vitay deactivates the user's account |
| User added to or removed from a group | Vitay updates the user's group membership accordingly |

<Note>
  Vitay provisions users into the hiring firm associated with your SCIM
  configuration. If your organisation uses multiple Vitay firms (for example, a
  parent and sub-accounts), Vitay's team will confirm which firm each SCIM
  connector maps to.
</Note>

## How it works

Vitay listens for SCIM events from your IdP (delivered via webhook). Each event
is recorded and processed asynchronously:

- **Create** — when a new member is assigned, Vitay creates the account. If the
  user first logs in via SSO before the SCIM event arrives, Vitay handles that
  gracefully (just-in-time creation at SSO callback takes precedence).
- **Update** — profile changes from your IdP are synced to the Vitay user
  record.
- **Delete / deactivate** — when a user is removed in your IdP, Vitay
  deactivates their account so they can no longer log in.

## What you provide to Vitay

To set up SCIM, share the following with Vitay's team:

- Your IdP's **SCIM connector type** (for example, Okta, Azure AD, or
  Google Workspace).
- The **email domains and groups** you want provisioned into Vitay.
- Any **attribute mappings** your IdP requires to correctly identify users
  (typically first name, last name, and email).

Vitay's team will provide the **SCIM endpoint URL** and **bearer token** to
enter in your IdP's SCIM configuration.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="A user was deprovisioned in the IdP but can still log in to Vitay">
    SCIM events are processed asynchronously. There may be a short delay between
    an IdP change and the deactivation taking effect in Vitay. If access persists
    beyond a few minutes, contact [support@vitay.io](mailto:support@vitay.io).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="A new user isn't appearing in Vitay after being assigned in the IdP">
    Confirm that the user's email domain matches the domain configured for your
    SCIM connector, and that the SCIM connector in your IdP shows a successful
    push (no error status). Share the IdP error log with Vitay's team if the
    issue persists.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="A user's name or email updated in the IdP but not in Vitay">
    Profile updates are handled by SCIM update events. Check your IdP's SCIM
    logs to confirm the update event was sent. If the event shows as delivered
    but Vitay hasn't synced, contact [support@vitay.io](mailto:support@vitay.io).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
