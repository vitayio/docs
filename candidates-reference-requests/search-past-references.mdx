---
title: "Search for past references"
description: "Find references already collected on a candidate or profile."
---

Vitay stores every reference that has been completed, even after a hire is made. If a candidate has been through your process before — or has an existing Vitay profile — you may already have references on file that you can review without sending a new request.

<Note>
  You need a recruiter (or higher) role to view references. References
  collected by other recruiters at your firm are visible to you unless your
  firm has restricted access by assigned user.
</Note>

## Find references on a candidate record

The quickest way to see past references for a specific candidate is through their profile in Vitay.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the candidate">
    Go to **Candidates** and search for the candidate by name, email, or internal ID.

    ![The Candidates list with the search bar](/images/candidates-list.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the References tab">
    On the candidate page, click the **References** tab. This lists every
    reference collected for this candidate — completed, pending, and manually
    added.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the reference">
    Click a completed reference to open it and read the answers and scores.
    See [Read a completed reference](/candidates-reference-requests/read-a-completed-reference)
    for a full walkthrough of the reference view.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Find references across all candidates

If you're not sure which candidate a reference is tied to, or you want to browse recently completed references across your book of business, use the global references view.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open References in the sidebar">
    In the left sidebar, click **References**. This shows a paginated list of
    all references your firm has collected, most recent first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter or search">
    Use the search or filter controls to narrow by candidate name, reference
    name, question set, or status. Completed references have a filled-in status
    indicator; pending ones are still awaiting a response.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open a reference">
    Click any row to open the individual reference and read its answers.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## References from a candidate's profile (reference reuse)

If a candidate has a Vitay profile — for example, because they previously submitted references for another job through Vitay — those references may already be on their profile and can be reused without collecting new ones.

To check:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the candidate">
    Go to **Candidates** and open the candidate record.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check for a linked profile">
    Look for a **Profile** section or link on the candidate page. If a profile
    is linked, it means this candidate has a Vitay history.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View profile references">
    Navigate to the linked profile to see any references collected under it.
    These are references submitted directly by the candidate's own network via
    a profile-based request — distinct from recruiter-initiated requests.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Profile references were collected via the candidate's own Vitay profile, not
  through your firm's question set. Review them to confirm they meet your
  firm's requirements before deciding whether to request new ones.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="I can't find a candidate I know exists">
    By default the Candidates list shows only active candidates. If the
    candidate was deactivated or anonymized, they may not appear in the default
    view. Ask your Vitay admin to check inactive records.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="The References tab is empty even though references were sent">
    References only appear here once they've been added (either by the
    candidate entering a reference's details, or by you adding one manually).
    If the request was sent but the candidate hasn't submitted any references
    yet, the tab will be empty. Check the **Reference requests** tab instead to
    see the pending request status.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
