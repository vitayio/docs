Vitay supports two forms of multi-factor authentication (MFA) to add a second layer of security to
your login. You can use a phone-based SMS code or a time-based authenticator app. These options
are configured in your account profile.

<Note>
  Your root recruiter may have enabled MFA as a requirement for your whole account. If that is
  the case, you will be prompted to set it up on your next login and cannot skip it.
</Note>

## SMS / phone MFA

SMS MFA sends a one-time code to your phone number each time you log in.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your profile">
    Click your name or avatar in the top-right corner and select **Profile** (or **Account
    settings**).

    ![SCREENSHOT: the account menu with the Profile option highlighted — placeholder](/images/placeholder.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the Security tab">
    Inside your profile, open the **Security** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable SMS MFA">
    Toggle on **SMS / phone MFA**. You need a phone number saved on your profile for this option to
    be available — if the toggle is greyed out, add your phone number first on the Profile tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save** (or **Submit**). The next time you log in, Vitay will send a code to your phone.
    Enter that code on the verification screen to complete sign-in.

    ![SCREENSHOT: the Security tab with SMS MFA toggle enabled — placeholder](/images/placeholder.png)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Authenticator app (TOTP)

Authenticator app MFA generates a time-based code in an app like Google Authenticator, Authy, or
Microsoft Authenticator. No phone signal is required once set up.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your profile">
    Click your name or avatar in the top-right corner and select **Profile**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the Security tab">
    Open the **Security** section inside your profile.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable authenticator app MFA">
    Toggle on **Authenticator app**. A QR code appears on screen.

    ![SCREENSHOT: the Security tab showing the QR code for authenticator app setup — placeholder](/images/placeholder.png)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scan the QR code">
    Open your authenticator app, tap the option to add an account, and scan the QR code displayed
    by Vitay. Your app will begin generating 6-digit codes that rotate every 30 seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The next time you log in, you will be asked for the current code from your
    authenticator app.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to expect at login

If MFA is active on your account, after entering your email and password you will see a second
screen asking for a verification code:

- **SMS MFA** — click **Send code** on the verification screen. A code is sent to your registered
  phone number. Enter the code and click **Verify**.
- **Authenticator app** — open your authenticator app, copy the current 6-digit code, and paste it
  into the verification screen.

## Disabling MFA

You can disable either MFA option from the same Security section of your profile by toggling it off
and saving. If your root recruiter has enforced MFA account-wide, you will not be able to disable it
yourself — contact your root recruiter or Vitay support.

## SSO / Single sign-on

If your organization uses SAML-based single sign-on, authentication is handled by your identity
provider and MFA is typically managed there rather than inside Vitay. See
[SSO / SAML](/security-it/sso-saml) for setup details.

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The SMS toggle is greyed out">
    You need a phone number saved on your profile before you can enable SMS MFA. Go to the
    **Profile** tab, add your phone number, save, then return to the Security tab.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="My authenticator codes aren't working">
    Authenticator codes are time-sensitive. Make sure your device's clock is set to the correct
    time (ideally synced automatically). If codes still fail, disable and re-enable the
    authenticator app option to generate a new QR code.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I've lost access to my MFA device">
    Contact your root recruiter. They can disable MFA on your account so you can log in and
    re-enroll with a new device.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="I'm being asked for MFA but never set it up">
    Your root recruiter may have enabled MFA as a firm-wide requirement. You will be walked
    through the setup flow automatically on your next login.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
